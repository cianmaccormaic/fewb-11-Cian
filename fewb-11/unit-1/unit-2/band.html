System Of A Down

System of a Down is an Armenian-American heavy metal band formed in Glendale, California, in 1994. Since 1997, the band consists of founding members

  • Serj Tankian (Lead vocals & Guitarist)
  • Daron Malakian (Lead guitarist & Vocals)
  • Shavo Odadjian (Bass & Backing Vocals)
  • John Dolmayan (Drums) who replaced original drummer Andy Khachaturian in 1997

A list of there albums in their release order.

1998 - System Of Down.
-System of a Down (1998) is the debut album by the American rock band System of a Down.
Known for its unique blend of metal, alternative rock, and Middle Eastern influences, the album showcases the band's signature mix of
aggressive riffs, complex rhythms, and politically charged lyrics. Themes in the album address topics like war, social issues, and personal struggles.
Songs like "Sugar" and "Spiders" highlight the band's chaotic yet melodic style, while lead singer Serj Tankian's distinctive vocals add an element of urgency and intensity.
Overall, the album helped establish System of a Down as a standout act in the late 90s alternative metal scene.

2001 - Toxicity.
-System of a Down's 2001 album Toxicity is a critically acclaimed blend of metal, alternative, and Armenian folk influences, marked by its chaotic, unpredictable style.
The album addresses themes such as political corruption, societal issues, war, and personal struggles. With tracks like "Chop Suey!", "Toxicity", and "Aerials",
the band mixes heavy riffs, intricate rhythms, and unique vocal delivery, blending dark, satirical lyrics with bursts of aggression and melody.
It became both a commercial and critical success, cementing System of a Down as one of the most distinctive acts in early 2000s metal

2002 - Steal This Album!.
-Steal This Album! is the second studio album by the Armenian-American metal band System of a Down, released in 2002.
The album features a mix of heavy metal, alternative rock, and elements of Middle Eastern music. It showcases a rawer, more experimental sound than their debut,
with politically charged lyrics addressing themes of war, corruption, social injustice, and personal turmoil. Many of the songs were originally recorded during the sessions for their first album but were held back.
Tracks like "Aerials," "Innervision," and "Boom!" highlight the band's unique blend of aggressive guitar riffs, eclectic rhythms, and intense, often surreal vocals.

2005 - Mezmerize.
-Mezmerize is the fourth studio album by System of a Down, released in 2005. The album blends elements of alternative metal, hard rock, and progressive metal, featuring politically charged and often surreal lyrics.
It explores themes such as war, corruption, and societal manipulation. The music is characterized by rapid shifts in tempo, complex song structures, and distinctive vocal performances by Serj Tankian and Daron Malakian.
Key tracks like "B.Y.O.B.," "Question!" and "Revenga" highlight the band's signature mix of aggression, melody, and satirical commentary. Mezmerize is known for its high energy, intense riffs, and unique sound,
solidifying System of a Down's position as one of the most innovative bands of the 2000s.

2005 - Hypnotize
-Hypnotize is the fifth studio album by American metal band System of a Down, released in 2005. It features a mix of alternative metal, hard rock, and elements of Middle Eastern music, with political and social commentary throughout.
The album addresses themes such as corruption, war, and human rights, while also exploring darker, surreal elements. Tracks like "B.Y.O.B." and "Hypnotize" stand out for their energetic sound and critical lyrics, offering both aggression and melody.
The album is often seen as a companion piece to its predecessor, Mezmerize, with both albums released within months of each other, and together they marked a creative peak for the band.
